Sickle Cell Disease

Sickle Cell Disease is the most common blood disease that children can inherit from their parents.

Signs and symptoms include:
Yellow eyes, mouth, palms, and other parts of the body.
Painful, Swelling of the hands and feet.
Easily fall sick.
Enlargement of the abdomen.
Frequent shortage of blood.


To find out if your child has sickle cell disease, take him/her to the health facility for laboratory tests as soon as possible.

*Note: Children with sickle cell disease easily get malaria, pnuemonia and other infections, so they should be protected.

Here are some tips on the carefor a child with sickle cell disease

Give breast milk only for the first 6 months of life for protection against infections.
After 6 months, give the child other foods in additon to the breast milk until he/she is at least 2 years old.
Protect the child from cold weather.
Let the child sleep under long lasting insecticide treated net every net.
Give the child daily folic acid and other medicines prescribed by health care providers.
Do not give iron tablets / syrup except when prescibed by the health care providers.
Give the child a lot of fluids to drink.
Always use warm water to bathe the child.
Keep all appointments with health care providers even if the child is well.
