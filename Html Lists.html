Health Messages During Pregnancy

1. Essential information and actions for pregnant women

1st Antenatal and Important Examination

  • Health care provider (midwife/doctor) will tell you when and how often you are recommended to come to ANC.
  • Always keep your appointments with health care provider but in case you feel unwell, see the provider even if it is not yet time for your next visit.
  • The following examinations will be conducted during ANC visits to ensure that you and your baby are in good conditions: Blood Pressure, Weight, Blood Test, HIV status, Urine and Stool Test, Ultrasound Scan.
  • You will be offered HIV test at ANC, so please take advantage of this.

Protecting your pregnancy

  1. Take iron and folic acid tablets every day during pregnancy to increase iron in your blood. Inadequate iron will lead to sickness (anaemia), which will make you and your baby weak, sick and tired.
  2. Taking iron tablets is not dangerous for your baby
  3. Take deworming tablets according to your test results. Worms can cause anaemia which leads to tiredness and poor health.
  4. Take tetanus diphtheria immunizationonce or twice during your antenatal period.

Prevention of Malaria

  1. Malaria can cause anaemia, preterm delivery and delivery of a small unhealthy baby. It can cause death of the mother or unborn baby.
  2. Sleep under a long lasting insecticide treated net to protect you an your unborn baby from malaria.
  3. Health care provider will give you anti-malaria tablets to take from the time you start feeling the movement of your baby.

2. How to maintain your health during pregnancy

Personal Hygiene

  • Bathe regularly with soap everyday.
  • Clean your teeth with tooth brush twice daily.

Resting, Relaxation and Sleeping

  • Get enough rest to restore your energy.
  • Get support from partner or other family members with household chores.

Safe Sex

  1. You may have sexual intercourse if this is not against your doctor's advice.

Exercise

  1. You are recommended to do simple exercise as long as you feel comfortable.

Things you should not do during pregnancy

  • Smoking
  • Take unprescribed drugs and concoctions
  • Taking alcohol
  • Engaging in strenous work
  • Lifting heavy loads

3. How to eat well during pregnancy

  1. Eat one extra meal each day (4 meals a day) and snack (such as fruits and biscuits) to stay healthy.
  2. The meals to be taken shoud be prepare from a variety of foods everyday. Eat more iorn-rich foods, e.g. meat, fis, etc.
  3. If you eat well, you will be healthier and your baby will grow healthier and stronger.
  4. Your family should provide a variety of foods for pregnant women. 4 meals a day and personal hygiene are also important for all your family memebers.

The 4 star diet

Staple

Grains such as maize, wheat, rice, millet and sorghum.

Roots and tubers such as cassava, yam, cocoyam, plantain and potatoes.

Legumes And Seeds

Beans, melon seeds, groundnuts and sesame.

Fruits And Vegetables

Mango, pawpaw, orane, banana, pineapple, watermelon, avocado.

Dark-green leaves, carrots, onoins, tomatoes, cocoyam leaves, cabbage, sweet potato and pumpkin.

Animal-Source Foods

Foods such as chicken, fish, liver, beef, eggs, milk and milk products.

  • Oil and fat such as oil seed, palm oil, fortified vegetables oil and butter added to vegetables and other foods will improve the absorbtion of some vitamins and provide extra energy.
  • You should use iodized salt.
  • You shoud drink lots of water and fresh juice as much as possible.
